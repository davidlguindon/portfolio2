2019-Present
Client: MutualAid

Design System Reboot

Overview
This project helps volunteer organizations deliver food and pharmacy goods to those who are at high risk for covid. It does this by helping volunteer coordinators assign tasks to volunteers willing to deliver goods to those in need. The project utilizes four separate applications that work together to receive, assign and track requests. It also facilitates delivering goods to the needy.
Role
I was part of a large group of designers who worked pro bono to help keep people safe during the covid outbreak. Karen Lo was the Lead Designer, while Miles Tompson was the Lead Developer and primary point of contact with the Stakeholders. I joined the group simultaneously with my UX Designer friends, Sarah Joo, Laura Green, Jordan Williams, and Abraham Friedman.
Problem
Our stakeholders have the problem that they cannot adequately scale up their volunteer delivery service with their current process. Our solution should help volunteer coordinators efficiently track and manage the tasks for delivering goods via volunteers.
Barriers
Ill leave this blank for now, but Ive always apreciated knowing some of the important constraints when looking over a case study. (honestly the biggest constraint was the lack of access to stakeholders, and potential users)
Solution
We created two mobile-based apps. One for volunteers to track deliveries and the other for recipients to submit requests. We also built a desktop-based dashboard for volunteer organizers to facilitate and monitor deliveries. The final piece is a website where people can learn about the service, donate, and request deliveries.
MutualAid used the design system to create four apps that worked together to form our solution.

Conclusion

The biggest lesson I learned from creating this design system is that it can significantly reduce the overall time spent on a project, especially on a project with a large team. I did not come into this project right at the start, and while we had a smaller set of components, it was not large enough for the project's scope, so most designs had to be reworked and looked at by several designers before it was ready to use. After the design system rework, most screens created by any designer were viable after minimal tweaking, speeding up the iteration process significantly.
